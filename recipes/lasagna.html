Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of wide, flat pasta interleaved with various fillings such as ragù (meat sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, and cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Originating in Italy during the Middle Ages, it has evolved into numerous regional variations across the country, each adding its unique twist to the basic recipe.

Whether it's the rich, meaty Bolognese style or the lighter, vegetable-packed versions found elsewhere, lasagna remains a symbol of Italian culinary excellence, celebrated for its comforting flavors and textures.

Ingredients

  • Lasgana noodles
  • Lean ground beef
  • Sweet Italian sausage
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic cloves, minced

Steps to Make Lasagna

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare your lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  3. In a large skillet, cook your chosen meats (if using) until browned.
  4. In another pan, sauté onions, garlic, and bell peppers until softened.
  5. Combine cooked meats, vegetables, and your preferred sauce in a baking dish.
  6. Layer lasagna noodles, ricotta cheese mixture, and mozzarella cheese in the baking dish.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until bubbly and golden brown.
  8. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before serving.
